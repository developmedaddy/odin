Arepas

Description

Arepas are small disc-shaped patties made of ground maize, or corn. They originate from northern South America, where they were enjoyed long before Columbus sailed the seas. They are delicious on their own, but may also be accompanied by a filling.

Ingredients

  • corn flour
  • water and/or plant milk
  • salt
  • oil
  • filling (optional)

Steps

  1. Heat the water or plant milk in a saucepan.
  2. Transfer the hot liquid to a bowl and add corn flour, mixing it to produce a dough-like consistency.
  3. Form the dough into small discs by hand and add them to a frying pan (high heat) along with a bit of oil.
  4. Turn after 2-3 minutes and add a sprinkle of salt.
  5. Cut them in half and fill them with something delicious, such as black beans, vegan cheese or a vegan meat substitute!
